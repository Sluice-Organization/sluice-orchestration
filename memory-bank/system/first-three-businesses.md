## Business 1: CleanLedger

**One-line description:** Automated bookkeeping and invoice tracking for cleaning businesses.

### Target Persona

Maria, 38, owns a residential and small commercial cleaning company with 4-12 employees. She tracks jobs in a mix of text messages, paper notebooks, and maybe a spreadsheet. She Googles things like "how to invoice cleaning clients" and "bookkeeping for cleaning business." She pays herself last, often forgets to bill recurring clients, and dreads tax season. She watches TikTok/Reels during breaks and follows other cleaning business owners for tips.

### Core Features

1. **Client & property database** - Store client info, property details (square footage, special instructions, access codes), and service history in one place.
2. **Recurring job scheduler** - Set up weekly/biweekly/monthly cleaning schedules with automatic reminders sent to both cleaners and clients.
3. **One-tap invoicing** - Generate and send branded invoices the moment a job is marked complete. Auto-attach before/after photos as proof of service.
4. **Expense tracking with receipt scanning** - Snap a photo of supply receipts (cleaning products, equipment). Auto-categorize expenses.
5. **Team pay calculator** - Track hours per cleaner per job, calculate pay (hourly or per-job), and generate pay stubs. Handle split payments for team jobs.
6. **Profit-per-client dashboard** - See which clients are profitable and which cost you money after factoring in drive time, supplies, and labor.
7. **Tax-ready reports** - One-click quarterly and annual reports formatted for Schedule C. Export to CSV for accountants.
8. **Client portal with online payments** - Clients get a link to view invoices and pay by card. Auto-chase overdue invoices with polite email/SMS reminders.

## Business 2: FieldLens

**One-line description:** Photo documentation and report generator for home inspectors.

### Target Persona

Dave, 45, is an independent home inspector or owns a 2-4 person inspection firm. He takes 200-400 photos per inspection, then spends 2-4 hours writing up the report in the evening. He currently uses a combination of a generic inspection template (Word or PDF), a photo folder on his phone, and maybe HomeGauge or Spectora (but finds them clunky and overpriced at $100-200/month). He Googles things like "home inspection report template" and "how to start a home inspection business." He loses evenings to report writing and wants that time back.

### Core Features

1. **Photo-first capture flow** - Walk through the property room by room. Tap to photograph, tap to tag (roof, HVAC, plumbing, electrical, etc.), and voice-dictate notes that get transcribed and attached to each photo.
2. **AI report draft** - After the walkthrough, the system generates a structured inspection report from your tagged photos and notes. Organized by section (exterior, interior, systems), with findings categorized as Major Defect / Minor Defect / Maintenance Item / Informational.
3. **Defect library with auto-descriptions** - Common defects (GFCI outlet missing, roof shingle damage, water staining on ceiling) come with pre-written descriptions and recommended actions. Tap to insert, edit if needed.
4. **Branded PDF report generator** - Output a professional PDF report with your logo, cover photo, table of contents, summary of findings, and full photo documentation. Clients and agents receive a shareable link.
5. **Client & agent CRM** - Track which real estate agents send you referrals. Log inspections by client, property, and referring agent. See your referral pipeline at a glance.
6. **Scheduling with confirmation** - Clients or agents book inspections through your booking page. Auto-send confirmation, reminder, and "report is ready" notifications.
7. **Agreement & payment collection** - Send the pre-inspection agreement for e-signature before the appointment. Collect payment at booking or upon report delivery.

## Business 3: PatchWork

**One-line description:** Job tracking and client communication hub for small remodeling and handyman businesses.

### Target Persona

Carlos, 41, runs a 2-6 person remodeling and handyman operation. He does kitchen remodels, bathroom renovations, deck builds, drywall, painting, and general repairs. He juggles 5-15 active jobs at any time, each at a different stage. He currently tracks everything in his head, a whiteboard, and text message threads with clients. He Googles "how to estimate remodeling jobs" and "contractor job management app." His biggest pain: clients constantly texting "when are you coming back?" and "what's the status?" He wants to look professional without hiring an office manager.

### Core Features

1. **Visual job board** - Kanban-style board showing all active jobs across stages: Lead / Estimate Sent / Approved / In Progress / Punch List / Complete / Paid. Drag and drop to update.
2. **Estimate builder** - Build itemized estimates with labor, materials, and markup. Pull from a library of common line items (demo, drywall per sq ft, paint per room, fixture install). Send as a branded PDF or web link. Client can approve with one click.
3. **Client update portal** - Each job gets a unique URL. Clients see current status, progress photos, upcoming schedule, and can message through the portal instead of texting your personal phone. Push updates to clients with one tap.
4. **Progress photo timeline** - Snap photos on-site and they auto-organize into a timeline for each job. Tag by room or phase. These photos feed into the client portal and become the final project gallery.
5. **Material & expense tracker** - Log material purchases per job. Snap receipts. See actual cost vs. estimated cost in real time so you know if a job is going over budget before it's too late.
6. **Simple scheduling** - Assign crew members to jobs by day. See a weekly calendar view of who is where. Crew members get a mobile view of their daily schedule with job address and notes.
7. **Invoice & payment** - Convert approved estimates to invoices. Send deposit requests, progress payments, and final invoices. Accept card payments through the portal.
8. **Lead capture form** - Embeddable form for your website or social media. New inquiries land directly on your job board as leads with contact info and project description.

## Summary: Portfolio View

| Business | Vertical | Price Range | Target Customers for $100K MRR | Market Size |
|----------|----------|-------------|-------------------------------|-------------|
| CleanLedger | Cleaning businesses | $49-179/mo | ~1,220 | 1.2M businesses |
| FieldLens | Home inspectors | $59-189/mo | ~1,050 | 35K+ inspectors |
| PatchWork | Remodeling/handyman | $59-199/mo | ~1,030 | 650K+ businesses |

**Why these three together:**
- **Risk diversification:** One large market (cleaning), one niche market (inspectors), one mid-size market (remodeling). If one vertical proves harder than expected, the others provide stability.
- **Shared infrastructure:** All three need the same backend components: user auth, subscription billing, photo upload/storage, PDF generation, email/SMS notifications, client-facing portals, and payment processing. Building one builds the foundation for all three.
- **Content flywheel overlap:** All three target small business owners who Google their problems, consume short-form video, and make purchasing decisions without talking to a salesperson. The same content production system (SEO articles + reels) works across all three.
- **Sequential launch recommendation:** CleanLedger first (largest market, simplest product), PatchWork second (large market, more complex product), FieldLens third (smallest market, but highest willingness to pay and clearest differentiation from competitors).
